Subhas Chandra Bose

1897 - 1945

Leader of the Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj)

Subhash Chandra Bose (1897–1945), fondly known as Netaji, was a pivotal, fiery leader in India's struggle for independence. Born in Cuttack, he was highly educated, clearing the ICS exam before choosing to fight against British rule. Though initially in Congress, he favored more militant, radical methods, famously breaking with Gandhi to later form the Forward Bloc. During WWII, he took a daring journey to Europe to seek help, eventually creating the Indian National Army (INA) with Japanese aid to fight for liberation from Southeast Asia. His iconic slogan, “Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom,” rallied thousands of Indians, both at home and abroad. As a brilliant strategist and passionate nationalist, he remains a revered hero. His bold actions, particularly the INA’s march toward India, dramatically accelerated the end of British rule. His legacy as a fearless patriot remains an inspiration, advocating for a free, sovereign India.
